New Zealand Work Guide

Professional & Skilled Work Pathways for Chinese Citizens

Relocating to New Zealand

Information based on current research for applicants from Mainland China wishing to live and work in New Zealand.

Available Visa Types for Mainland Chinese Citizens

Mainland Chinese citizens primarily apply under the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) and Specific Purpose Work Visa. Please note that there is currently no Working Holiday Scheme available for PRC passport holders.

Main Work Routes

AEWV

The main work visa requiring the employer to be accredited and to have an approved Job Token for the position.

Specific Purpose

For short-term, specialized roles, professional engagements, or specific events in New Zealand.

General Requirements

Valid Passport (for full duration of stay)
Job offer from an accredited New Zealand employer
Authenticated qualifications & relevant experience
Full medical certificate (chest X-ray & exam)
Proof of financial means & good character checks

Typical Process

1
Determine Visa Type

Identify the correct visa route (usually AEWV) based on your job offer components.

2
Accredited Job Token

Your NZ employer must provide a Job Token link before you can apply for the AEWV.

3
Online Application

Register and apply online via Immigration New Zealand. Be prepared to upload numerous supporting documents.

4
Processing in China

Attend a NZVAC in Beijing, Shanghai, or Guangzhou for biometrics. Processing typically takes 3–8 weeks.

New Zealand Visa Application Centres – China

Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou (via VFS Global)

Mon – Fri (Appointment required)

immigration.govt.nz

Service Note

VFS Global manages the reception of applications and biometric data across major hubs in China. The eVisas are issued by Immigration NZ officials.
