New Zealand Work Guide

Living & Working Rights for Australian Citizens in NZ

The Trans-Tasman Connection

A comprehensive guide for Australian citizens wishing to live and work in New Zealand.

Trans-Tasman Travel Arrangement

As an Australian citizen, you have the right to live, work, and study in New Zealand without the need for a visa. You are generally granted a Resident Visa automatically upon arrival at the New Zealand border, provided you meet character requirements. There are no age limits, no quotas, and no need to apply for anything before you fly.

Your Rights in New Zealand

Unlimited Work Rights

Take up any employment or start a business immediately. No sponsorship or work permit required.

Permanent Residence

Live anywhere in Aotearoa indefinitely. You are considered a resident for most domestic purposes.

Social Support

Access to public healthcare and certain social security benefits (usually after a waiting period).

What You Need

Valid Australian Passport
Good character (no serious criminal record)
New Zealand Traveller Declaration (online form)
IRD Number (for tax, apply after arrival)
Medicare card (for Reciprocal Health Care)

Arrival Checklist

1
Book Your Flight

Simply book a flight to NZ. No visa application or NZeTA is required for Australian citizens.

2
Cross the Border

Present your Australian passport. You'll be granted a resident visa automatically at the electronic gates or by an officer.

3
Apply for IRD Number

Apply online for a tax (IRD) number so you can be taxed correctly by your employer.

4
Start Working

You are free to start any job immediately. Show your passport to your employer as proof of work rights.
