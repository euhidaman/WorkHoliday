USA Work Guide

J-1 Exchange Visitor Programs for Austrian Citizens

Relocating to the United States

A step-by-step roadmap for Austrian students and professionals wishing to participate in the J-1 Intern or Trainee program.

J-1 Visa Program

Austrian citizens can apply for the J-1 Exchange Visitor Visa to work in the USA as an Intern (for students or recent grads) or a Trainee (for professionals with 1-5 years exp). You must be sponsored by a designated US organization (Sponsor) during your stay.

Visa Categories

J-1 Intern

For currently enrolled post-secondary students or those who graduated within 12 months. Max duration 12 months.

J-1 Trainee

For professionals with a degree and 1 year work exp, or 5 years exp if no degree. Max duration 18 months.

Summer Work Travel

Seasonal work program for students during their summer holidays. Limited to 4 months.

Requirements

Valid Austrian Passport
DS-2019 Form (from US Sponsor)
English Language Proficiency (B2 or better)
Proof of Funds for initial stay
Health Insurance meeting US Govt standards

Application Process

1
Find a US Sponsor

Contact a US-designated sponsor organization (e.g., CIEE, InterExchange) to get approved for the program.

2
Receive DS-2019

The sponsor issues the DS-2019 form after reviewing your internship/traineeship offer (DS-7002 Training Plan).

3
Pay SEVIS Fee

Pay the I-901 SEVIS fee online and complete the DS-160 visa application form.

4
Embassy Interview

Schedule and attend an interview at the US Embassy in Vienna. Visa approval is usually determined on the spot.

Service Note

The Consular Section handles all non-immigrant visa (J-1) queries. Appointments are required for all visa applicants. Ensure you bring your DS-2019 to the interview.
