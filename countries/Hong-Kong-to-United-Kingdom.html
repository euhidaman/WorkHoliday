United Kingdom Work Guide

BNO, Youth Mobility & Career Migration for HK Residents

Relocating to Great Britain

A comprehensive guide for Hong Kong residents wishing to live and work in the United Kingdom.

Available Visa Types for Hong Kong Citizens

Hong Kong citizens can apply for the BN(O) Visa (if eligible), the Youth Mobility Scheme (requires a ballot selection), or the Skilled Worker Visa. The Youth Mobility Scheme is for those aged 18-30.

Visa Categories

BN(O) Visa

For BNO status holders. Stay for 2.5 or 5 years. Full work rights. Pathway to citizenship. No age limit.

Youth Mobility Scheme

For HKSAR passport holders aged 18-30. Requires successful selection via the ballot system held twice a year (February and July). Max 2-year stay.

Skilled Worker

Standard professional route for those with a job offer from a licensed UK employer and a CoS.

General Requirements

Valid HKSAR or BN(O) Passport
Proof of Savings (£2,535 for YMS)
Negative TB Test Certificate (Mandatory for HK residents)
Certificate of Sponsorship (from HK Gov, HKSAR YMS only)
Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) payment

Preparation Steps

1
TB Medical Test

Book a test at a UKVI-approved clinic in HK. You must have the result before you apply for the visa.

2
Online Application

Submit your application on GOV.UK. Most HK residents can use the app to verify their identity digitally.

3
Fees & Health Levy

Pay the relevant visa fee and the IHS (£1,035/year for BN(O), £776/year for YMS).

4
Receive Status

Once approved, BNO visa holders get a digital status. YMS applicants might get a vignette sticker to collect a BRP in the UK.
