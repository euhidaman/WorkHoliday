Mainland China Work Guide

Work & Residency Pathways for Hong Kong Residents

Relocating to Mainland China

Information based on current research for Hong Kong residents wishing to live and work in the Mainland.

Mainland Travel Permit (Home Return Permit)

Hong Kong residents of Chinese nationality typically use the Mainland Travel Permit for Hong Kong and Macao Residents to enter and work in the Mainland without a visa. Since 2018, the requirement for a specific "Taiwan, Hong Kong and Macao Citizens' Work Permit" has been abolished.

Main Work Routes

Residence Permit

For HK residents living in the Mainland for >6 months. Provides access to social services, social security, and housing funds.

GBA Talent Scheme

Special professional and tax incentives for HK talents working in the Greater Bay Area (GBA) cities like Shenzhen and Guangzhou.

Direct Employment

HK residents can be directly employed by Mainland entities using their Home Return Permit. No separate work visa is required.

General Requirements

Mainland Travel Permit (Home Return Permit)
Valid Employment Contract (under Mainland labor law)
Social Security Registration (Mandatory for employees)
Health Check (Required for certain industries)
Police Registration Form (for Residence Permit)

Typical Process

1
Enter with Home Return Permit

Entry is seamless via kiosks/e-gates. No visa application is needed for Chinese nationals from HK.

2
Sign Employment Contract

Ensure the contract complies with Mainland labor laws and handles social security (Wujing).

3
Apply for Residence Permit

If staying for >6 months, apply for the Residence Permit for HK, Macao, and Taiwan Residents.

4
Social Security Setup

The employer must register the employee in the local social security and housing fund systems.

Bureau of Public Security (Exit-Entry Administration)

Local bureaus in destination cities (e.g. Shenzhen, Guangzhou)

Handles Residence Permits and local registrations

Service Note

For HK residents who are NOT of Chinese nationality, the standard Foreigner Work Visa (Z-Visa) process applies as for other international citizens.
