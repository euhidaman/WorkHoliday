Austria Work Guide

Work & Residency Information for German Citizens (EU Free Movement)

Working in Austria as an EU Citizen

As a German citizen, you enjoy the right to live and work in Austria without a visa or work permit under the EU Principle of Free Movement.

EU Freedom of Movement

German citizens do not require a visa or work permit to enter, live, or work in Austria. You have the same rights as Austrian citizens regarding employment, working conditions, and social benefits.

Registration Requirements

Valid German ID Card or Passport
Local Address Registration (Meldezettel)
Registration Certificate (Anmeldebescheinigung)
Employment Contract or Proof of Self-employment
Comprehensive Health Insurance (e.g., ÖGK)

Mandatory Steps

1
Local Address Registration

Register your address at the local Meldeamt (in the Town Hall or District Office) within 3 days of moving into an apartment.

2
Obtain a Social Security Number

Your Austrian employer will register you with the Social Security (ÖGK), and you will receive your e-card.

3
Apply for Registration Certificate

If staying for longer than 4 months, you must apply for an "Anmeldebescheinigung" at the local migration authority (Magistrat/BH).

4
Tax Registration

Ensure you are correctly registered with the Austrian Tax Office (Finanzamt) for income tax purposes.

Service Note

Austrian and German social security systems are closely linked. You should check the details of your health insurance and pension contributions when moving across the border.
