Austria Work Guide

Work & Residency Pathways for Australian Citizens

Relocating to Austria

Information based on current research for Australian citizens wishing to live and work in the Republic of Austria.

Available Visa Types for Australian Citizens

Australian citizens have access to the Working Holiday Visa, the Red-White-Red (RWR) Card, and the EU Blue Card. The RWR Card is a points-based system for skilled immigrants.

Main Work Routes

Red-White-Red Card

The primary route for skilled workers. Points-based (Age, Education, Experience, Language). Requires a binding job offer with a minimum salary.

Working Holiday

Allows Australians (18-30) to work and travel for up to 1 year. Popular for short-term and seasonal employment.

Jobseeker Visa

For "Very Highly Qualified Workers" to search for a job in Austria for 6 months. Requires high points (70/100).

General Requirements

Valid Australian Passport
Employer Declaration (for RWR Card)
University Degree (Points for Education)
Language Skills (German A1+ or English)
Proof of Local Accommodation

Typical Process

1
Visa Application

Apply for the RWR Card at the Austrian Embassy in Canberra or a Consulate in Australia.

2
AMS Verification

Your points and job offer are verified by the Public Employment Service (AMS) in Austria.

3
Visa D Issuance

Receive a Visa D for entry if you are not already in Austria under a visa-free scheme.

4
Collection of Permit

Enter Austria and collect your RWR residence permit from the local authority (Magistrat or Bezirkshauptmannschaft).

Service Note

Australian citizens can enter Austria visa-free for up to 90 days. You may apply for the RWR card during this period within Austria, but it is recommended to start the process at the Embassy.
