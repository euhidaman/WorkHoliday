Austria Work Guide

Work & Residency Pathways for New Zealand Citizens

Relocating to Austria

Information based on current research for New Zealand citizens wishing to live and work in the Republic of Austria.

Available Visa Types for New Zealand Citizens

New Zealand citizens can apply for the Working Holiday Visa (limited to 6 months), the Red-White-Red Card, or the EU Blue Card.

Main Work Routes

Red-White-Red Card

The points-based migration system for skilled workers. Based on factors like qualifications, experience, age, and language knowledge.

Working Holiday

For New Zealanders aged 18-30. Allows you to work and travel for up to 6 months (shorter than other nationalities due to bilateral terms). The scheme is designed for cultural exchange and short-term work.

Jobseeker Visa

For "Very Highly Qualified Workers" to stay for 6 months to search for a job. Requires high points (70/100).

General Requirements

Valid New Zealand Passport
Binding job offer with minimum salary
University Degrees (English/German translations)
Language scores (German A1+ or English)
Police Certificate (Authenticated NPC)

Typical Process

1
Visa Submission

Submit your Red-White-Red Card application at the Austrian Embassy in Australia (as there is no full visa embassy in NZ) or via a Consulate.

2
AMS Points Review

Qualifications and job details are reviewed by the Austrian Public Employment Service (AMS).

3
Visa D / Arrival

If approved, you receive a Visa D for entry. NZ citizens can also enter visa-free to finalize local steps.

4
Resident Permit Issuance

Collect your RWR residence permit card from the local authority (Magistrat/BH) in Austria.

Service Note

Documents from New Zealand must be authenticated by the Department of Internal Affairs (Apostille) before they are accepted by Austrian authorities.
